## 1. Total Addressable Market (TAM)

### 1.1 How Many Websites Exist Globally?

As of early 2026, approximately **1.34–1.38 billion websites** exist globally, but only **~200–206 million are active** (roughly 15% of all registered domains).

> Source: [Hostinger](https://www.hostinger.com/tutorials/how-many-websites-are-there), [Reboot Online](https://www.rebootonline.com/website-statistics/), [Siteefy](https://siteefy.com/how-many-websites-are-there)

For TAM purposes, the relevant universe is **active websites** — sites with regular content or business operations: **~200 million**.

### 1.2 What Percentage Need Accessibility Compliance?

The WebAIM Million Report (2025) found that **94.8% of the top 1 million websites have detectable WCAG failures** — down only marginally from 95.9% in 2024. This means:

- Less than 5% of websites are currently meeting basic WCAG compliance standards.
- The average non-compliant page has **51 distinct accessibility errors**.

> Source: [WebAIM Million 2025](https://webaim.org/projects/million/), [WebAIM Million 2024](https://webaim.org/projects/million/2024)

For businesses with a legal obligation to comply (commercial entities, government contractors, healthcare, education, finance), a conservative estimate is **30% of active business websites** have an immediate compliance need or legal exposure. This yields:

- ~200M active websites × ~40% are business-operated = **~80 million business websites**
- Of those, ~95% are non-compliant = **~76 million websites that need remediation or auditing**
- Immediate addressable need (legal exposure, regulated, or scaling) = conservatively **20–30%** = **~15–20 million websites**

### 1.3 Average Audit Price

Based on market research:

| Service Tier | Price Range | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Automated scan / report | $99–$500 | Solopreneur, micro-business |
| AI-assisted audit (report + findings) | $300–$1,200 | SMB, 5–50 pages |
| Manual WCAG conformance audit | $1,500–$5,000 | SMB, standard site |
| Agency full audit (manual + VPAT) | $5,000–$30,000 | Enterprise |
| SaaS platform audit + remediation | $30,000–$100,000+ | Large enterprise |

> Source: [DigitalA11Y](https://www.digitala11y.com/how-much-does-a-web-accessibility-audit-cost/), [Accessible.org](https://accessible.org/cost-website-accessibility-audit/), [Custom Web Audits](https://customwebaudits.com/how-much-does-an-ada-accessibility-audit-cost/)

For an AI-powered solo operator targeting SMBs, a realistic price point is **$500–$1,500**, with a blended average of **$800**.

### 1.4 TAM Calculation

```
TAM = Active business websites needing audits × average audit price

Conservative: 15,000,000 × $800 = $12.0B (theoretical full-market refresh)
Annual TAM (assuming 3-year audit cycle): $12.0B ÷ 3 = ~$4.0B/year

Market research cross-check:
- Digital accessibility market: $1.42B (2025) → $3.24B (2034), CAGR 8.6%
  [Source: Straits Research]
- Website accessibility software segment: $212M (2024), CAGR 9.6%
  [Source: Research and Markets]
- Services (audits, remediation, consulting) represent the larger slice
```

**TAM Estimate: $2.5B – $4.0B annually (global, all audit/compliance services)**

For this analysis, using **$2.59B** as the primary figure, consistent with the professional services layer of the Grand View Research digital accessibility software market, adjusted for services vs. software ratio.

> Source: [Grand View Research](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/digital-accessibility-software-market-report), [Straits Research](https://straitsresearch.com/report/digital-accessibility-market)

### 2.3 EU Businesses — European Accessibility Act (EAA) Wave

The EAA took effect **June 28, 2025**. Key facts:

- Applies to any business (including non-EU) selling products or services online to EU consumers.
- Exempts only micro-enterprises (<10 employees AND <€2M revenue).
- The EU has ~27 million SMEs; roughly 23 million are micro-enterprises, leaving ~4 million larger SMEs in scope.
- US, UK, and AU companies with EU customers must also comply.
- Penalties: up to €100,000 or 4% of annual revenue per member state.
- First enforcement actions began within **days** of the June 28 deadline (French visual impairment associations notified 4 major retailers in early July 2025).

> Source: [European Commission EAA](https://commission.europa.eu/strategy-and-policy/policies/justice-and-fundamental-rights/disability/european-accessibility-act-eaa_en), [ADA Title III on EAA](https://www.adatitleiii.com/2025/08/european-accessibility-act-poses-new-challenges-for-us-companies-with-customers-in-the-eu/)

The EAA represents a significant new demand catalyst for English-speaking businesses with EU exposure (US/UK/AU ecommerce, SaaS, fintech).

### 3.2 Cold Outreach Funnel

Industry benchmarks for B2B cold email (2025):

| Stage | Rate | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Open rate | ~39% average | Snov.io, Martal.ca |
| Reply rate | 4–5.1% average | Belkins.io |
| Meeting/call booked | 1–2% of contacts | Standard B2B benchmark |

> Source: [Martal.ca B2B Cold Email Stats](https://martal.ca/b2b-cold-email-statistics-lb/), [Belkins.io](https://belkins.io/blog/cold-email-response-rates)

**Outreach model for Year 1:**

```
Cold outreach capacity (solo): 50 contacts/day × 5 days × 50 weeks = 12,500 contacts/year

  Conservative: 125 × $750 = $93,750

  Conservative: 125 × $1,000 = $125,000
  Optimistic: 188 × $1,000 = $188,000
  Stretch: 250 × $1,000 = $250,000
```

### 3.3 Demand-Signal Targeting (High-Conversion Approach)

Rather than cold outreach to random SMBs, targeting businesses that have **just received an ADA demand letter** or are named in accessibility lawsuits dramatically improves conversion:

- 4,028 federal + state lawsuits projected for 2025 (full year)
- Each defendant is an immediate, motivated buyer
- ~500 additional businesses receive pre-suit demand letters for every lawsuit filed (industry estimate)
- Total motivated buyers in 2025: **~2M demand letters + 4K lawsuits = ~2M warm leads**

At a 2–3% conversion on law-triggered outreach: **40,000–60,000 potential clients nationally** — far exceeding solo capacity.

**The bottleneck is delivery capacity, not demand.**

### 3.4 SOM Calculation

```
SOM (Year 1) = Solo operator capacity × realistic win rate

Capacity ceiling: 500–800 audits/year
Realistic Year 1 acquisition: 60–180 clients (ramp-up, reputation building)

At $3,000 avg (full audit + remediation report, mid-tier SMB):
  Low: 60 × $3,000 = $180,000
  High: 180 × $3,000 = $540,000

At $750 avg (report-only, high volume):
  Low: 60 × $750 = $45,000
  High: 180 × $750 = $135,000

Recommended pricing strategy: $1,500–$3,000 (quality positioning, not race-to-bottom)
Mid estimate: 120 clients × $2,000 = $240,000
```

**SOM (Year 1): $180K – $540K** (recommended: target $240K at $2K avg, 120 clients)

## 4. ADA Lawsuit Trends

### 4.1 Annual Volume

| Year | Federal Lawsuits | State Lawsuits | Total | Change |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2022 | ~2,387 | ~1,400 | ~3,787 | — |
| 2023 | ~2,824 | ~1,237 | ~4,061 | +7.2% |
| 2024 | ~2,452 | ~1,600 | ~4,052 | -0.2% |
| 2025 H1 | ~1,200 est. | ~814 | ~2,014 | +37% vs H1 2024 |
| 2025 Full Year (proj.) | — | — | ~4,500–5,000 | +12–23% |

> Source: [Accessibility.Works 2024 Review](https://www.accessibility.works/blog/ada-lawsuit-trends-statistics-2024-summary/), [EcomBack 2025 Mid-Year Report](https://www.ecomback.com/ada-website-lawsuits-recap-report/2025-mid-year-ada-website-lawsuit-report), [AudioEye 2025 Analysis](https://www.audioeye.com/post/website-accessibility-in-2025/)

### 4.2 Key Trends

- **Serial plaintiffs dominate**: 188 plaintiffs drove all 2,014 H1 2025 lawsuits.
- **Geographic shifts**: Illinois surged 745% (28 → 237 cases); Florida nearly doubled to become #2 state.
- **Overlay widget backlash**: 22.6% of H1 2025 lawsuits explicitly targeted sites *with* accessibility widgets installed — widgets are not a legal shield.
- **Federal vs. state split**: Federal filings down 13% in 2024, but state filings increasing sharply; California and New York still dominate but Illinois and Florida gaining fast.
- **ADA demand letters**: For every lawsuit filed, industry experts estimate 50–500 demand letters are sent that never reach court (settled out-of-court, typically $5K–$25K settlements).

### 4.3 Estimated Total Demand Letters (2025)

```
4,000 lawsuits × ~100 demand letters per lawsuit = ~400,000 demand letters/year
```

This represents a massive pool of businesses that are either currently panicked or recently remediated — both are warm markets for audit services.

## 6. Competitive Landscape

### 6.1 Current Market Pricing Tiers

| Competitor Type | Price Range | Weakness |
|---|---|---|
| Overlay widgets (accessiBe, UserWay) | $49–$999/mo | Don't actually achieve compliance; facing lawsuits |
| Automated scan tools (WAVE, axe) | Free–$500 | Only detect ~13% of WCAG issues automatically |
| Boutique agencies | $1,500–$30,000 | Too expensive for SMBs; slow delivery |
| Large firms (Level Access, Deque) | $30,000–$100,000+ | Enterprise only |
| AI-assisted (emerging, 2025–2026) | $300–$2,000 | Entering market now |

> Source: [TestParty Pricing Comparison](https://testparty.ai/blog/testing-tools-pricing-comparison), [Accessible.org AI Audit Trends](https://adabook.medium.com/accessibility-services-ai-driving-down-wcag-audit-costs-in-2026-80e2576a08e1)

### 6.2 AI-Assisted Audit Gap

The key market opportunity:

- **Automated tools alone**: Only detect 13% of WCAG 2.2 AA criteria reliably; cannot detect 42% at all.
- **Manual audits**: Expensive ($1,500–$5,000+), slow (2–4 weeks), inaccessible to SMBs.
- **AI-assisted hybrid**: Can achieve 60–80% detection accuracy at 1/5th the cost, with turnaround in days.

This creates a **$300–$1,500 sweet spot** currently underserved — SMBs who need more than a free scan but cannot afford a $3,000 agency audit.

> Source: [Accessible.org AI Hybrid Audit Projections](https://accessible.org/ai-hybrid-wcag-audits/)

## 9. Conclusion

The web accessibility audit market is **large, growing, and structurally underserved at the SMB level**. The combination of:

1. **~95% of websites still non-compliant** (WebAIM Million 2025)
2. **ADA lawsuits up 37% in H1 2025** — with demand letters estimated at 400K+/year
3. **EAA enforcement active as of June 2025** — adding millions of EU-facing businesses to the compliance queue
4. **AI cost compression** — making $500–$2,000 audits economically viable where $5,000+ agency audits were previously the only option
5. **Market growing at 8.6–9.6% CAGR** with no sign of slowing

...creates a clear window for a solo AI operator to build a $180K–$540K Year 1 business, scaling to $500K–$2M+ in Years 2–3 as reputation, referrals, and tooling efficiency compound.

The bottleneck is **not demand** — it is **delivery capacity and client acquisition systems**.
