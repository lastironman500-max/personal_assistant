# Website Accessibility Scan Preview
## Saved by the Dress — savedbythedress.com
**Scan Date:** March 15, 2026

### Overall Results
- **Pages Scanned:** 1 (homepage)
- **Total Issues Found:** 25
- **Critical/Error Issues:** 25

### Top 3 Issues Found (Preview)

**Issue 1: Sale/Crossed-Out Price Images Have No Alt Text**
- What's wrong: Decorative "crossed-out" price images used throughout the featured collection (indicating sale pricing) have no alt attribute. Screen readers either skip them silently or read raw filenames like "cross-out.png."
- Where: Featured product collection grid — appears on nearly every product card
- Why it matters: Shoppers using screen readers cannot tell whether a product is on sale. For an ecommerce store where sale promotions drive conversions, this is a direct barrier to purchase decisions for customers with visual impairments.

**Issue 2: Social Media Links Are Invisible to Screen Readers**
- What's wrong: Footer links to Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, and Pinterest use only icon images with no alt text. Screen readers cannot describe where these links go.
- Where: Site footer social media icons
- Why it matters: Social proof and community are central to fashion ecommerce. Customers using assistive technology are cut off from these channels — and the links themselves provide no navigational context.

**Issue 3: Currency/Localization Form Has No Submit Button**
- What's wrong: The localization form (used to select currency or region) has no keyboard-accessible submit button. Users who cannot use a mouse have no way to submit this form.
- Where: Toolbar and footer localization forms
- Why it matters: Keyboard-only users — including many people with motor disabilities — cannot change currency or locale, which may also affect checkout completion.

### What This Means for Saved by the Dress
At roughly 400,000 cumulative orders, Saved by the Dress has built real scale — and with that comes both a larger addressable audience and greater exposure to ADA Title III web accessibility claims, which have increased sharply in the fashion and apparel sector. Ensuring that every shopper, regardless of ability, can browse products, understand pricing, and complete checkout is both the right thing to do and a sound business decision.
