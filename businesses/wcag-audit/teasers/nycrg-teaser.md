### Top 3 Issues Found (Preview)

**Issue 1: Reservations Button Duplicated in a Way That Breaks Assistive Technology**
- What's wrong: The "Reservations" button appears twice on the page with the same HTML id value. This violates a fundamental HTML rule and causes screen readers to behave unpredictably — skipping one button entirely or reading content out of order.
- Where: Primary reservations call-to-action button (appears in both the header and a callout section)
- Why it matters: For a restaurant group, the reservation button is arguably the most important interactive element on the site. If screen reader users cannot reliably locate or activate it, they're booking elsewhere.

**Issue 2: Email Signup Button Has the Same Duplicate ID Problem**
- What's wrong: The "Email Signup" button also uses a duplicated id, again appearing in two locations. Same consequence: screen readers lose track of which element is which, and keyboard navigation may loop or fail.
- Where: Header callout and inline section email signup buttons
- Why it matters: Email list growth is a direct marketing channel for any restaurant group. Inaccessible signup flows exclude a segment of potential guests before they've even visited.

**Issue 3: Form Fields Inaccessible to Keyboard and Screen Reader Users**
- What's wrong: The reCAPTCHA textarea embedded in the reservation/newsletter form has no accessible label. Additionally, a form on the page has no programmatic submit button, meaning keyboard-only users cannot complete the form at all.
- Where: Reservation and newsletter signup forms
- Why it matters: A guest who cannot use a mouse — due to a motor disability, for example — cannot submit a reservation request or sign up for updates. This closes a key guest acquisition channel.
