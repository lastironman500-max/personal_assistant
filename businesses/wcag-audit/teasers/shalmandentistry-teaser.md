### Top 3 Issues Found (Preview)

**Issue 1: Entire Navigation Menu Is Unreadable**
- What's wrong: All top-level navigation links — About, Services, Dental Implants, Invisalign, Before & After, Common Problems, Contact — have a text contrast ratio of 1:1. The text is rendered in the same color as its background, making it invisible.
- Where: Primary site navigation menu
- Why it matters: A patient with low vision visiting your site for the first time cannot read any of the navigation labels. They cannot find the services page, contact information, or appointment booking — the most critical actions on a dental practice website.

**Issue 2: Hero Section Text Is Also Invisible**
- What's wrong: The homepage headline ("Welcome to Shalman Dentistry"), the practice description, and the feature bullet points all share the same 1:1 contrast ratio problem. The entire above-the-fold content block is unreadable to low-vision users.
- Where: Homepage hero / banner section
- Why it matters: The headline and value proposition are the first things a prospective patient should see. If they can't read it, the site fails to convert at the very first impression for a significant portion of visitors.

**Issue 3: Google Maps Iframe Has No Title**
- What's wrong: The embedded Google Maps iframe used to show your office location has no title attribute. Screen readers cannot identify or describe it.
- Where: Contact/location section of the homepage
- Why it matters: Patients with visual impairments who use screen readers are trying to find your address. The embedded map is inaccessible, and without a title it is announced as a nameless embedded object — unhelpful and disorienting.

### What This Means for Shalman Dentistry
A dental practice's website serves as the front door for new patients — many of whom may be older adults or individuals with visual or motor impairments who have significant dental care needs. The contrast failures found here affect core content across the entire homepage, not just peripheral elements. Healthcare-adjacent businesses are also subject to increasing legal scrutiny around digital accessibility. These issues are addressable with targeted CSS and code changes.
