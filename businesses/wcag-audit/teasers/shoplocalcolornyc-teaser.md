### Top 3 Issues Found (Preview)

**Issue 1: Store Logo Links Nowhere for Screen Reader Users**
- What's wrong: The logo at the top of the page is a linked image with no alt text. Screen readers announce it as "link, image" with no description — users have no idea where it leads or what it represents.
- Where: Site header logo (links to homepage)
- Why it matters: For blind and low-vision shoppers navigating your site, this is disorienting from the very first element they encounter. It signals immediately that the site was not built with them in mind.

**Issue 2: Search Bar Is Completely Inaccessible**
- What's wrong: The search input field has no accessible label, and the search submit button contains only a decorative icon with no text or aria-label. Neither element is usable via keyboard or screen reader.
- Where: Site search bar (appears in both mobile and desktop navigation)
- Why it matters: Search is one of the primary ways shoppers find products. If a customer using assistive technology cannot use your search bar, they're likely to leave and shop elsewhere.

**Issue 3: Product and Collection Images Missing Alt Text**
- What's wrong: Multiple homepage images — including featured collection photos and the Instagram-feed style shop tiles — have no alt attribute at all. Screen readers skip them or read out raw file names.
- Where: Featured collection grid and homepage promotional images
- Why it matters: For an image-driven boutique like Local Color NYC, product photography is central to the shopping experience. Customers who rely on screen readers receive no information about what's being sold.

### What This Means for Local Color NYC
Brooklyn shoppers with visual impairments, motor disabilities, or cognitive differences represent real potential customers — and right now your homepage creates significant barriers for them at every step, from navigation through product discovery. For a boutique built on community and local connection, an inaccessible website is a missed opportunity to serve the full breadth of your neighborhood.
